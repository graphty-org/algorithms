Degree Centrality Explained

Degree centrality measures how many direct connections a node has.

Nodes with more connections are considered more central or important in the network.

How Degree Centrality Works:

Degree centrality is the simplest centrality measure:

  • Count the number of edges connected to each node
  • Normalize by dividing by the maximum possible degree (n-1)
  • Higher values indicate more connected nodes
  • In social networks: more friends = higher degree centrality

Formula: degree_centrality(v) = degree(v) / (n-1)
